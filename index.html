Philosophy

A symbiosis between the nature that surrounds us and the passing of age. In our kitchen, the aim is to go back to the origins of our ancestors when meals were defined by the geographical position and the season upon it.

The Concept

ΚΛΙΛΜO is all about seasonality and terroir. Our menu changes once every three months. In this scenario, nature is the true artist. As Chefs, we just prepare the main stage for it to shine at its own pace through a particular form of cuisine.

The Chef

Chef Radu Ionescu spent 7 years abroad, in the city of London, where he pursued a career in business & marketing that radically shifted, leading the young lad to the aspiring world of gastronomy. The work experience started from small neighborhood restaurants, to 5-Star hotels and ending up in the kitchen of the notorious Chef Ollie Dabbous at his 1 Michelin Star extravaganza. Chef Radu graduated from Le Cordon Bleu with a Grand Diplôme de Cuisine et Pâtisserie followed by a Diploma in Culinary Management, becoming an Alumni of this iconic institute, in 2015.

The Restaurant

ΚΛΙΛΜO is genuinely defined by the imperfect perfection. The cracks, the efforts, the sacrifices we desire to share with each and every guest. Our table tops are custom-made from very old Romanian wood, rescued before being turned into coal.

The Ingredients

From plant to plate. We’ve been doing it since 2001; it’s in our DNA. You can’t put a price on the inspiration and freshness of ingredients handpicked from the garden. Working the soil, planting, watering and harvesting: we do them all with pleasure.

As a guest, we invite you to step into our world and allow us to influence yours.
Contemplate this.

Seasonal Menu

UNCHARTED ORIGINS. ROMANIAN TRIBUTE.
A symbol of change. A concept of contrast. A pure art form of nostalgia. A story that underlines the fear of mediocrity and the tendency to exploit the peak of potential and constantly strive for the absolute.
Those dishes are some of our all time favourite:

"Sunny Day in Transylvania"

A crepe of active charcoal encasing saffron pilau, a lazy snail, sourdough soil, algae caviar, organic micro-tomatoes and roasted kapia & dried tomatoes sauce.

As the first leaves of autumn touch the warm ground, shy and gentle textures of the earth inspire us continuously. With a touch of bronze.

"Aphrodisiac"

Oyster of coconut and jasmine, black tea, cassava pearls

This is slowly becoming our perfect flower, blooming day by day in its quest for the absolute. An aphrodisiac of senses and fulfillment. Showcased now, just before this weekend’s OYSTER FEAST. Offer us a glimpse of your own perception and allow us to twist it without boundaries of patterns. Allow synesthesia to kick in and embrace your taste buds from that first little bite. Look closer, things are changing.

"Nose to tail"

Lobster tail cured in Mangalica pure fat, crustaceans & tomato seeds sauce, foam of pickled elderflowers

From the ideology of nose to tail, we take upon in synergy the noble lobster tail and cure it in the humble Mangalica fat. To that, add a splash of classic bisque from the shells and a foamy air of pickled elderflowers.

"Ostropoda"

Charred octopus, ripe tomatoes, smoked garlic

A classic Romanian sauce, derivative of ‘ostropel’ with a sea creature to enhance the ripeness of summer heritage tomatoes and confit garlic. Some say a classic with a twist. We say it’s worth a taste.

"Taste of Tomorrow"

Pumpkin. 9 different textures.

Some might see a Halloween themed dish. We see something else. A philosophy of the simple idea that one day, due to the over-consumption and misfit globalization, we may end up running out of ingredients. And it would be a shame. However, this dish underlines the versatility of ingredients such a humble pumpkin. In this plate you will notice 9 different textures of pumpkin. Salt and pepper. And hints of local dairy. The change won’t happen if we won’t.

"The Bunger"

Our signature #BUNGER. A fluffy milk bun with charcoal. Filled with a decadence of grade 9 wagyu, pan fried foiegras and local truffles.

Let us repeat that again: WAGYU, FOIE GRAS & TRUFFLES. In a milk bun.
